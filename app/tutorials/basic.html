Welcome to cycleCAD
Learn the basics of browser-based parametric 3D CAD modeling

cycleCAD is a modern, open-source CAD modeler that runs directly in your browser. Like Fusion 360 or OnShape, it lets you create parametric 3D parts using sketches, extrusions, and advanced operations — all without installing desktop software.

What You'll Learn
This tutorial covers the essentials: navigating the 3D view, drawing sketches, creating your first part, and exporting your work. Plan on 20-30 minutes.

By the end, you'll have built a simple rectangular box from scratch. After that, the Intermediate tutorial will teach you more complex shapes and operations.

Interface Overview
Tour the main UI layout and panels

cycleCAD is laid out like a professional CAD tool with a central 3D viewport, toolbars, and side panels.

⚙️
Main interface showing 3D viewport, toolbar, and side panels

Key Areas:

  • Top Toolbar — Sketch, Extrude, Revolve, Fillet, Chamfer, Pattern, Assembly, and other tools. Tabs let you switch between View, Analyze, Create, Export, and AI Tools.
  • Left Panel — Model Tree showing all features and components. You can rename, suppress, or delete features here.

  • Right Panel — Properties/parameters tab, AI Chat tab, and Guide tab. Change dimensions and material properties here.
  • Status Bar (bottom) — Shows selection info, operation status, and keyboard hint.

Your First 3D Part
Build a simple 50mm tall box step by step

Let's make a rectangular box. The pattern is: sketch a rectangle → extrude it → done. In cycleCAD, every 3D part starts as a 2D sketch.

Step-by-Step:

  1. Click "Sketch" in the toolbar. You'll switch to 2D sketch mode (the viewport turns blue).
  2. Click the rectangle tool (or press R). Your cursor will show a crosshair.
  3. Draw a rectangle by clicking two opposite corners: first at (0, 0), then at (100, 80). The grid helps you snap to round numbers.
  4. Exit sketch mode by pressing Esc or clicking the checkmark. The rectangle is now a flat sketch.
  5. Click "Extrude" in the toolbar. A panel appears on the right asking for the height.
  6. Type 50 in the height field. You'll see the box rise to 50mm tall in the viewport.
  7. Click OK. Your first 3D part is complete!
Tip: Undo is Your Friend
Made a mistake? Press Ctrl+Z to undo. You can undo as many times as needed.
Note: Units
cycleCAD uses millimeters (mm) by default. All dimensions are in mm.

Navigating the 3D Viewport
Master camera control with your mouse and keyboard

The 3D viewport uses industry-standard controls. Once you learn them, they'll feel natural.

🎯
3D viewport with your box displayed

Mouse Controls:

  • Rotate: Left-click and drag. The model spins under your cursor.
  • Pan: Right-click and drag (or Shift + left-drag). The model slides sideways.
  • Zoom: Scroll your mouse wheel up (zoom in) or down (zoom out).

Preset Views (Press Number Keys):

  • 1 — Front view
  • 2 — Top view
  • 3 — Right-side view
  • 7 — Isometric (3D perspective)
  • 0 (zero) — Fit all (zoom to see entire model)
Warning: Orthographic vs Perspective
Preset views (Front, Top, Right) are orthographic (flat, no perspective). Isometric (7) is a 3D view. Switch between them to understand your model's shape.

Drawing Sketches
Master 2D sketch tools and constraints

Sketches are the foundation of 3D modeling. Every extrusion, revolution, or sweep starts with a 2D sketch. cycleCAD provides tools for drawing lines, rectangles, circles, and arcs with automatic constraint detection.

Sketch Tools:

  • Line Tool (L) — Click to place points. Press Esc to finish.
  • Rectangle Tool (R) — Click two opposite corners to draw a rectangle.
  • Circle Tool (C) — Click center, then drag to set radius.
  • Arc Tool (A) — Click start, center, and end points.
  • Polyline Tool — Draw connected line segments. Press Esc to finish.

Grid and Snapping:

  • Toggle Grid: Press G to show/hide the grid.
  • Snap to Grid: When you draw, your cursor automatically snaps to the nearest 10mm grid point.
  • Precision Drawing: Type exact coordinates in the properties panel if you need exact dimensions.
Tip: Automatic Constraints
When you draw a vertical or horizontal line, cycleCAD automatically applies vertical/horizontal constraints. When two lines meet at the same point, it applies a coincident constraint. These constraints keep your sketch from accidentally moving.

Basic Operations
Extrude and revolve to create 3D shapes

Two operations form the foundation of most models: extrude (pull a sketch into a 3D box) and revolve (spin a sketch around an axis).

Extrude:

  • Create a sketch and exit sketch mode.
  • Click "Extrude" in the toolbar (or press E).
  • In the right panel, enter the height (e.g., 50 mm).
  • Optionally, enable "Two Direction" to extrude both sides equally.
  • Click OK. Your sketch is now a 3D solid.

Revolve:

  • Create a sketch of half your final shape (e.g., a triangle for a cone).
  • Click "Revolve" in the toolbar (or press R).
  • Select the axis of rotation (usually a vertical or horizontal line in your sketch).
  • Set the angle to 360° for a complete rotation, or less for a partial cone/wedge.
  • Click OK. Your sketch is now a 3D solid of revolution.
Note: Sketch Orientation
When you extrude, the sketch extrudes perpendicular to its plane (the Z direction if you drew on the XY plane). When you revolve, the axis must lie on the sketch plane.

Feature Tree
Manage and organize your model's features

The Feature Tree (left panel) shows every sketch, extrusion, and operation in your model, in the order you created them. It's like a recipe: each step depends on the previous one.

🌳
Feature tree showing Sketch1 and Extrude1 as a hierarchical list

Tree Operations:

  • Click a feature: Highlights that feature in the 3D view.
  • Double-click a feature: Edits it (change the extrude height, for example).
  • Right-click a feature: Context menu with Rename, Suppress, Delete options.
  • Suppress: Temporarily hides the feature and all features after it. Useful for testing changes.
  • Delete: Removes the feature and updates the model accordingly.
Tip: Naming Features
Right-click any feature and choose "Rename" to give it a meaningful name like "Main Body" or "Mounting Hole". This makes your tree easier to understand.

Exporting Your Model
Save your work in multiple formats

Once you've created your model, you'll want to save it. cycleCAD supports several export formats for different purposes: 3D printing, CAM, visualization, or collaboration.

Export Formats:

  • STL (Stereolithography) — 3D printable mesh format. Use this for 3D printing, simulation, or sharing a solid model.
  • OBJ (Wavefront OBJ) — Universal 3D mesh format. Good for visualization in other software or game engines.
  • glTF 2.0 (GL Transmission Format) — Modern 3D format with support for materials and animations. Best for web viewers and AR.
  • cycleCAD JSON — Save your full feature history and parameters. Load it back to edit later.

How to Export:

  1. Click the "Export" tab in the toolbar.
  2. Choose your format (STL, OBJ, glTF, or JSON).
  3. Click the format button. A file dialog appears.
  4. Name your file and click Save.
Warning: Lost Parametric Data
When you export to STL/OBJ/glTF, the file contains only the final 3D geometry (a "snapshot"). You can't edit the sketch or parameters later. To preserve your work, export as cycleCAD JSON first, then export as STL when you're ready to share.

Keyboard Shortcuts Reference
Speed up your workflow with keyboard commands

Memorizing a few shortcuts will dramatically speed up your modeling. Here's a complete reference.

General:

Shortcut Action
Ctrl+Z / Cmd+Z Undo last action
Ctrl+Y / Cmd+Shift+Z Redo
Ctrl+S / Cmd+S Save (cycleCAD JSON)
Ctrl+O / Cmd+O Open saved file
Esc Cancel current operation, exit sketch mode
Del Delete selected feature or part

Sketch Tools:

Shortcut Action
S Enter Sketch mode
L Line tool
R Rectangle tool
C Circle tool
A Arc tool
G Toggle grid on/off

3D Operations:

Shortcut Action
E Extrude sketch
V Revolve sketch
F Fillet edges
H Chamfer edges
P Pattern (array)
B Boolean operation (union, cut, intersect)

Viewport Navigation:

Shortcut Action

2 Top view

What to learn after the basics

Congratulations! You've learned the fundamentals of cycleCAD. You can now:

  • Create sketches with lines, rectangles, circles, and arcs
  • Extrude and revolve sketches into 3D solids
  • Navigate the 3D viewport with your mouse
  • Use the feature tree to organize your model
  • Export your work in multiple formats

Ready to learn more? The Intermediate tutorial covers:

  • Fillets and Chamfers: Smooth and bevel edges for realistic parts
  • Boolean Operations: Union, cut, and intersect multiple solids
  • Patterns: Create rectangular and circular arrays of features
  • Assemblies: Combine multiple parts into an assembly with constraints
  • Advanced Sketches: Constraints, driven dimensions, and symmetric geometry

After that, the Advanced tutorial unlocks:

  • Sweep: Pull a profile along a 3D path
  • Loft: Blend between multiple profiles
  • Sheet Metal: Bend, flange, and unfold metal parts
  • AI Copilot: Generate designs and CAM code with natural language
