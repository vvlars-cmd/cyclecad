Copilot Features — Detailed

1. Text-to-CAD

Describe a part in plain English → get a fully parametric 3D model. "Create a 50mm spur gear with 20 teeth and a 10mm bore" → sketch + extrude + pattern + hole.

vs. MecAgent: works in our tool, not as a plugin. vs. Aurorin: open-source + browser.

2. Natural Language Editing

"Make the hole 2mm larger" → finds the hole feature, updates the dimension, rebuilds. "Add a 3mm fillet to all edges" → selects edges, applies fillet.

Context-aware: knows your current selection, feature tree, and assembly.

3. Smart Autocomplete

Predicts your next operation based on what you've done. Just extruded a boss? Suggests "Add fillet?" with optimal radius. Building a bracket? Suggests hole pattern.

Learns from your workflow patterns within the session.

4. Manufacturability Check

Analyzes geometry for DFM issues: wall thickness too thin, draft angles missing, undercuts that can't be machined, tolerances that are too tight for the material.

Runs automatically on every feature, not as a separate step.

5. Cost Estimation

Estimates manufacturing cost from geometry + material + process. "This part costs ~$12 to CNC from 6061 aluminum, ~$3 to 3D print in PLA, ~$0.80 injection molded at 10K units."

Integrated with McMaster pricing for standard parts.

6. Photo → Part Identification

Take a photo of a physical part → AI identifies it → suggests McMaster-Carr match → imports CAD model. Already have Gemini Vision in ExplodeView, extend to real photos.

Camera on phone → identified → ordered → in your assembly. End to end.

7. Standards Compliance

Auto-check against DIN/ISO standards. Flag non-standard bolt sizes, verify thread specs, check material compatibility. Already have DIN/ISO identifier in ExplodeView.

vs. MecAgent: same feature but free and built-in.

8. Assembly Intelligence

"How do I disassemble the motor?" → AI reads assembly tree + mate constraints → generates step-by-step removal instructions with tool requirements.

Already have assembly instructions in ExplodeView. Copilot makes them conversational.
